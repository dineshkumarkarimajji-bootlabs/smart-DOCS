India is the seventh-largest country in the world by land area.
It has a population of over 1.4 billion people, making it the second-most populous country.
The capital of India is New Delhi.
India has 28 states and 8 union territories.
The official languages of India include Hindi and English, but there are over 22 recognized regional languages.
The national flag of India has three horizontal stripes: saffron, white, and green, with a blue Ashoka Chakra in the center.
India is known for its diverse culture, festivals, and traditions.
Taj Mahal in Agra is one of the Seven Wonders of the World.
The Indian economy is one of the fastest-growing economies globally.
India gained independence from British rule on August 15, 1947.
The Ganges and Yamuna are two of the most important rivers in India.
The Himalayas, including Mount Kanchenjunga, the third-highest peak in the world, are in northern India.
Indian cuisine is diverse and includes dishes like biryani, dosa, samosas, and paneer tikka.
India is the birthplace of major religions such as Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, and Sikhism.
Bollywood, based in Mumbai, produces the largest number of films per year globally.
The Indian rail network is one of the largest in the world.
Cricket is the most popular sport in India.
India has a rich history of science and mathematics, including contributions from Aryabhata and Bhaskara.
The Indian Parliament consists of the Rajya Sabha and Lok Sabha.
Major tourist destinations include Jaipur, Kerala, Goa, Varanasi, and Himachal Pradesh.
India has a rich wildlife diversity, including tigers, elephants, and rhinoceroses.
The Indian space program, ISRO, has achieved major milestones including the Mars Orbiter Mission.
The Indian Constitution is the longest written constitution of any country.
The currency of India is the Indian Rupee (INR).
Festivals like Diwali, Holi, Eid, Christmas, and Navratri are widely celebrated across the country.
